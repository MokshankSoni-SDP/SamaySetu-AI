Connect Your Google Calendar

Follow these 5 steps — takes about 5 minutes. We'll guide you through each one.

Step 1 of 5 — One-time setup
Enable the Google Calendar API
Google requires you to "turn on" the Calendar feature before anything can use it. This is a one-click action inside Google Cloud Console.

1. Click the button below to open Google Cloud Console.
2. At the top, click Select a project → New Project. Name it SamaySetu-Calendar and click Create.
3. In the search bar, type Google Calendar API.
4. Click the result, then click the blue Enable button.

✦ Come back to this page once you have clicked Enable.
Open Google Cloud Console ↗
Step 2 of 5 — Part A
Create a Service Account
Think of this like creating a staff pass that lets SamaySetu access your calendar — without needing your Google password.

1. Click the button below to open the Service Accounts page.
2. Click + Create Service Account at the top.
3. In the name field, type samaysetu-sync.
4. Click Create and Continue, then Done.
Open Service Accounts ↗
Step 2 of 5 — Part B
Copy the service account email
In the list you will see a row for your new account. Copy the email and paste it below — you will need it in Step 4.
✓ Saved — we'll show this again in Step 4.
Step 2 of 5 — Part C
Download your JSON security key
Now you need to download the actual key file. Follow these exact clicks:
1
In the Service Accounts list, find your samaysetu-sync row. Click the three dots (⋮) under the Actions column.
2
Click Manage keys.
3
Click the Add key button, then Create new key from the dropdown.
4
Make sure JSON is selected (it says "Recommended" below it). Click Create.
5
A file downloads automatically — keep this file safe. This is your private key.
Step 3 of 5
Find Your Calendar ID
The Calendar ID is like an address for your specific calendar inside Google.

1. Open Google Calendar in your browser.
2. On the left side, find your calendar name and click the three dots (⋮) next to it.
3. Click Settings and sharing.
4. Scroll down to Integrate calendar. You'll see the Calendar ID — it looks like an email address.
5. Copy it and paste it below.

Your primary calendar ID is usually just your Gmail address.

Open Google Calendar ↗
Step 4 of 5
Share Your Calendar with SamaySetu
Now you need to give the service account permission to read and write your calendar.

1. Open Google Calendar and click the three dots (⋮) next to your calendar.
2. Click Settings and sharing.
3. Scroll to Share with specific people or groups.
4. Click + Add people and groups.

7. Click Send.
