Connect Your Google Calendar

Follow these 4 steps — takes about 5 minutes. We’ll guide you through each one.

Step 1 of 4 — One-time setup
Enable the Google Calendar API
Google requires you to “turn on” the Calendar feature before anything can use it. This is a one-click action inside Google Cloud Console.

1. Click the button below to open Google Cloud Console.
2. At the top, click Select a project → New Project.
    Name it SamaySetu-Calendar and click Create.
3. In the search bar, type Google Calendar API.
4. Click the result, then click the blue Enable button.

✦ Come back to this page once you have clicked Enable.
Open Google Cloud Console ↗
Step 2 of 4 — Part A
Create a Service Account
Think of this like creating a staff pass that lets SamaySetu access your calendar — without needing your Google password.

1. Click the button below to open the Service Accounts page.
2. Click + Create Service Account at the top.
3. In the name field, type samaysetu-sync.
4. Click Create and Continue, then Done.

You will see your new account listed with an email ending in @...iam.gserviceaccount.com
Open Service Accounts ↗
Step 2 of 4 — Part B
Copy the service account email
In the list you will see a row for your new account. The email column shows something like:
[email protected] Copy that email and paste it in the box below — you will need it in Step 4.
Saved — we'll show this again in Step 4.
Step 2 of 4 — Part C
Download your JSON security key
Now you need to download the actual key file. Follow these exact clicks:
1
In the Service Accounts list, find your samaysetu-sync row.
At the far right of that row, click the three vertical dots (⋮) under the Actions column.
2
A small dropdown menu appears with options like Manage details, Manage permissions, Manage keys...
Click Manage keys.
3
You are now on the Keys page for your service account.
Click the Add key button, then click Create new key from the dropdown.
4
A popup appears asking for the key type.
Make sure JSON is selected (it says "Recommended" below it).
Click Create.
5
A file downloads automatically to your computer — it is named something like
your-project-name-xxxxxxxx.json
Keep this file safe — this is your private key.
6
Open the downloaded file:
— Windows: right-click the file → Open with → Notepad
— Mac: right-click → Open with → TextEdit

Press Ctrl+A (Windows) or Cmd+A (Mac) to select all,
then Ctrl+C / Cmd+C to copy the entire contents. You will paste this in the final step.
Step 3 of 4
Find your Calendar ID
The Calendar ID tells SamaySetu which of your calendars to use.

1. Open Google Calendar in your browser.
2. On the left, hover over the calendar you want → click the three dots (⋮).
3. Click Settings and sharing.
4. Scroll down to Integrate calendar.
5. Copy the Calendar ID — it looks like your Gmail address or a long code.
Open Google Calendar ↗
Paste your Calendar ID here
Saved.
Step 4 of 4 — Most important!
Share your calendar with SamaySetu
Without this step, SamaySetu cannot see or edit your calendar — this is the step most people miss.

1. In Google Calendar, open the same Settings and sharing page from Step 3.
2. Scroll up to Share with specific people or groups.
3. Click + Add people and paste this email:
—
You did not enter your service account email in Step 2. Please go back and paste it there first.
4. Set permission to Make changes to events.
5. Click Send.
⚠ "Make changes" is greyed out? This can happen on school or work Google accounts. Contact your IT admin, or use a personal Gmail calendar instead.
Final step
Paste your JSON file and connect
Open the JSON file from Step 2 with Notepad or TextEdit. Select all (Ctrl+A / Cmd+A), copy, and paste below.
